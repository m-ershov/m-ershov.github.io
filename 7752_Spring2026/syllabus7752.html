Course content

The course will consist of 3 main parts:
  • Modules and advanced linear algebra (approximately 4 weeks).
  • Representations theory of finite groups (approximately 4 weeks).
  • field and Galois theory (approximately 6 weeks).
The material in the first part will be taken primarily from Chapters 10-12 of Dummit and Foote (DF). More specifically, the plan is to cover 10.4, the entire Chapter 12 and parts of 11.5, time permitting. I will assume familiarity with the content of Sections 11.1-11.4 as this material is discussed in standard undergraduate linear algebra and Sections 10.1-10.3 which were covered in Algebra-I in Fall 25. The relevant DF chapters for the second part are 18 and 19; however, we may not be using DF for representation theory. Finally, the material on field theory and Galois theory we will cover is mostly discussed in Chapters 13-14 of DF, though our order of my exposition will be quite different. The current plan is to cover 13.1, 13.2, 13.4, 13.5, parts of 13.6, 14.1-14.4 and parts of 14.5 and 14.6.
